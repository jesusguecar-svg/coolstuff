# Phase II-A: Premium Prep Package Architecture
## Texas General Lines Life, Accident & Health Agent Exam (Texas)

> **Scope lock (Phase II-A):** This artifact defines package architecture only. It does **not** draft full lesson prose, final quiz items, study calendar logic, or tracker logic.

---

## SECTION 1: PACKAGE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 Why split the package into two books (National vs Texas)
**Decision:** Build two primary learning books:
1) **License Exam Manual (National/General Knowledge, Domains 1–9)**
2) **Texas State Law Supplement (Domains 10–13)**

**Why this is mandatory for a premium product:**
- **Different cognitive skill:** National domains are concept/application-heavy; Texas domains are statute/rule precision-heavy.
- **Different update cycles:** State-law revisions can be published in supplement releases without rewriting the national manual.
- **Lower confusion risk:** Students stop mixing “general insurance principle” with “Texas-specific legal requirement.”
- **Commercial parity:** Mirrors high-performing prep products (core manual + state addendum).

### 1.2 How exam weights control depth and production effort
Use blueprint weights as the controlling variable for:
- chapter page budget,
- instructional media minutes,
- checkpoint emphasis,
- QBank volume,
- remediation priority.

#### Weight-to-Effort Tiers
- **Tier A (15–16%)**: Domains 1, 2, 5, 6 → maximum instructional depth and most QBank volume.
- **Tier B (12–14%)**: Domains 3, 10 → operational/legal precision with high scenario practice.
- **Tier C (8%)**: Domains 4, 9 → moderate depth with application exercises.
- **Tier D (3–7%)**: Domains 7, 8, 11, 12, 13 → concise but exam-targeted coverage.

### 1.3 Unit design model: Concept + Memory + Trap
Each unit is architected with three built-in layers:
- **Concept layer:** “how and why” insurance mechanics work.
- **Memory layer:** deadlines, notices, options, required provisions, eligibility triggers.
- **Trap layer:** distractor patterns (timing swaps, role confusion, qualifier misses, exception misuse).

### 1.4 Why Checkpoints, QBank, and Mastery sit in different stages
- **Checkpoint exams:** stage-gates after unit clusters (progression control).
- **QBank drills:** continuous targeted practice by weak tag (diagnostic/repair engine).
- **Mastery exams:** late-stage full simulation of pressure and weighted topic mixing.

### 1.5 Failure-risk reduction logic (first-attempt pass strategy)
This architecture reduces first-attempt failure risk by:
- forcing mastery of high-weight domains first,
- preventing random-drill overconfidence,
- separating Texas law for clarity,
- requiring threshold-based progression,
- mandating remediation loops before high-stakes simulation.

## SECTION 2: CORE PACKAGE COMPONENTS

For each component: purpose, timing, content, exclusions, detail standard.

### 2.1 License Exam Manual (National)
- **Purpose:** Primary instruction for Domains 1–9.
- **When used:** Phase 1 of learning sequence.
- **Contains:** structured units, scenario examples, diagrams, key terms, mini reviews.
- **Does not contain:** primary Texas law instruction.
- **Detail:** highest in Tier A/B domains.

### 2.2 Texas State Law Supplement
- **Purpose:** State-specific legal instruction for Domains 10–13.
- **When used:** After national core (then revisited before mastery).
- **Contains:** black-letter law summaries, plain-English meaning, citation tables, legal trap notes.
- **Does not contain:** duplicate generic product teaching.
- **Detail:** concise but exact; citation-aware formatting.

### 2.3 Cram Sheets (National + Texas)
- **Purpose:** Last-mile compression and memory stabilization.
- **When used:** after each major cluster + final review window.
- **Contains:** one-page and two-page high-yield sheets, timelines, compare/contrast blocks.
- **Does not contain:** long narrative exposition.
- **Detail:** rapid-scan format only.

### 2.4 Unit Mini Reviews (embedded)
- **Purpose:** immediate retrieval and misconception detection.
- **When used:** every subunit and unit ending.
- **Contains:** short mixed checks (concept/memory/trap).
- **Does not contain:** full checkpoint simulation.
- **Detail:** 5–12 items per subunit.

### 2.5 Checkpoint Exams
- **Purpose:** progression gates after coherent clusters.
- **When used:** end of each cluster.
- **Contains:** multi-unit mixed exam + domain/tag diagnostics.
- **Does not contain:** full final simulation behavior.
- **Detail:** 35–60 items depending on cluster weight.

### 2.6 Topic-Based QBank
- **Purpose:** targeted drill and remediation by tags.
- **When used:** throughout; mandatory after weak checkpoint/mastery results.
- **Contains:** domain/subtopic/difficulty/trap-tagged items with back-link rationales.
- **Does not contain:** replacement for reading sequence.
- **Detail:** high-volume bank with adaptive presets.

### 2.7 Video Review
- **Purpose:** clarify systems, workflows, and contrasts visually.
- **When used:** after first read and after checkpoint analysis.
- **Contains:** short concept explainers with visual maps.
- **Does not contain:** legal citation detail as the only medium.
- **Detail:** 4–9 minute clips.

### 2.8 Audio Review
- **Purpose:** portable repetition for recall-heavy material.
- **When used:** in reinforcement windows after reading/practice.
- **Contains:** memory cues, trigger phrases, quick compare lists.
- **Does not contain:** diagram-dependent topics as sole modality.
- **Detail:** 3–7 minute clips.

### 2.9 Mastery Exam Layer
- **Purpose:** final readiness simulation.
- **When used:** only after checkpoints and remediation.
- **Contains:** exam-weighted, timed, mixed-difficulty forms.
- **Does not contain:** in-question teaching.
- **Detail:** full-length simulation standard.

### 2.10 Optional Instructor/Coaching Layer
- **Purpose:** human-guided intervention for plateau/failure points.
- **When used:** after weak checkpoints, before mastery retakes.
- **Contains:** remediation prescriptions, office-hour coaching, confidence triage.
- **Does not contain:** replacement for student independent work.
- **Detail:** lightweight but high-impact overlays.

## SECTION 3: LICENSE EXAM MANUAL ARCHITECTURE (NATIONAL)

### 3.1 National Unit Set (N1–N8)
1. **N1 Life Policy Types & Design Logic** (Domain 1)
2. **N2 Life Riders, Provisions, Options, Exclusions** (Domain 2)
3. **N3 Application, Underwriting, Policy Delivery** (Domain 3 + Domain 9 bridge)
4. **N4 Retirement & Other Life Concepts** (Domain 4)
5. **N5 Health Policy Types & Plan Design** (Domain 5)
6. **N6 Health Provisions, Clauses, Riders** (Domain 6)
7. **N7 Social Insurance** (Domain 7)
8. **N8 Other Concepts + Field Underwriting Integration** (Domain 8 + Domain 9 consolidation)

### 3.2 Recommended sequencing rationale
- **N1→N2:** product before contract detail.
- **N3→N4:** operations before retirement-adjacent concepts.
- **N5→N6:** health product before health contract detail.
- **N7→N8:** compact public-program domain then integrated final application.

### 3.3 Depth standards by unit
- **Longest/most detailed:** N1, N2, N5, N6.
- **Second-tier depth:** N3.
- **Medium:** N4, N8.
- **Compact high-yield:** N7.

### 3.4 Asset placement inside each unit
- **Opening page:** objective map + domain weights + “why this matters.”
- **Mid-unit:** worked examples and trap callouts.
- **End of each subunit:** mini review (5–8 items).
- **End of unit:** unit quiz (10–15 items).
- **End of selected units:** cram sheet insert.

### 3.5 National cram sheet placement
- **After N2:** Life Contracts & Riders cram sheet.
- **After N6:** Health Provisions & Riders cram sheet.
- **After N8:** National Final Rapid Recall sheet.

## SECTION 4: TEXAS SUPPLEMENT ARCHITECTURE

### 4.1 Texas chapter set (TX1–TX4)
1. **TX1 Common Texas Life & Health Statutes/Rules** (Domain 10, weight 14)
2. **TX2 Texas Life-Only Statutes/Rules** (Domain 11, weight 6)
3. **TX3 Texas A&H-Only Statutes/Rules** (Domain 12, weight 7)
4. **TX4 Texas HMO Statutes/Rules** (Domain 13, weight 3)

### 4.2 Chapter internal format (repeatable template)
Each TX chapter uses three fixed blocks:
1. **Black-letter law block** (requirement-level statement)
2. **Plain-English exam takeaway block**
3. **Exam trap block** (“commonly confused with…”) 

### 4.3 Citation-driven table placement
At the end of every TX subchapter, include a **rule table** with:
- requirement,
- applicability,
- trigger/timeframe,
- exceptions,
- citation placeholder.

### 4.4 Texas cram sheet placement
- **After TX1:** Common TX Life/Health law sheet.
- **After TX3:** Life-vs-A&H Texas distinctions sheet.
- **After TX4:** HMO one-page high-yield sheet.

### 4.5 Emphasis priority (weight + confusion risk)
1. TX1 (highest)
2. TX3 (high confusion risk)
3. TX2 (precision-heavy)
4. TX4 (small but unique vocabulary)

## SECTION 6: CHECKPOINT ARCHITECTURE

### 6.1 Number of checkpoints
**5 checkpoint exams** (CP1–CP5).

### 6.2 Coverage map
- **CP1 (Life Core):** N1 + N2
- **CP2 (Operations/Retirement):** N3 + N4
- **CP3 (Health Core):** N5 + N6
- **CP4 (National Integration):** N7 + N8 + cumulative national blend
- **CP5 (Texas Law):** TX1 + TX2 + TX3 + TX4

### 6.3 Item-count architecture (recommended)
- CP1: 45 items
- CP2: 35 items
- CP3: 45 items
- CP4: 40 items
- CP5: 50 items

### 6.4 What checkpoints measure
- cluster comprehension,
- application ability,
- trap resistance,
- readiness to progress.

### 6.5 Progression thresholds
- **Pass to continue:** ≥75% overall **and** no covered domain <65%.
- **Borderline:** 70–74% = remediation then retake alt form.
- **Not ready:** <70% = mandatory remediation cycle.

### 6.6 Weak-performance flow
1. assign QBank by weak domain + trap tag,
2. require focused reread + cram sheet,
3. complete reinforcement media,
4. retake parallel checkpoint form.

### 6.7 Distinction by assessment role
- **Checkpoint:** cluster gate.
- **QBank:** targeted drill engine.
- **Mastery:** full readiness simulation.

## SECTION 7: QBANK ARCHITECTURE

### 7.1 Required question tags
- `jurisdiction` (national/texas)
- `domain` (1–13)
- `unit_id` (N1–N8, TX1–TX4)
- `subtopic`
- `difficulty` (easy/medium/hard)
- `trap_type` (definition_swap, timing_trap, role_confusion, exception_misread, qualifier_ignore)
- `concept_type` (concept/memory/application/hybrid)
- `cognitive_skill` (recall/compare/apply/diagnose)

### 7.2 Quiz generation modes
1. **Unit Drill** (single unit)
2. **Weak-Area Repair** (lowest-performance tags)
3. **Exam-Weighted Mix** (blueprint-distributed)
4. **Trap Immunity** (single trap-type focus)

### 7.3 Rationale design standard
Every rationale must include:
1. why correct is correct,
2. why at least one likely distractor is wrong,
3. source pointer (`Manual/Supplement > Unit > Section`),
4. trap-type annotation.

### 7.4 QBank role boundaries
- QBank is **diagnostic + reinforcement**, not primary content delivery.
- Unlock unit-tag drilling only after initial unit completion.
- Route repeated misses back to source reading before additional drilling.

### 7.5 Interaction with checkpoints/mastery
- Checkpoint reports auto-generate weak-tag playlists.
- Mastery reports auto-generate final remediation playlists by domain + trap.

## SECTION 8: VIDEO AND AUDIO ARCHITECTURE

### 8.1 Reinforcement by cluster

**Cluster A (N1–N2: Life Core)**
- Video: policy-family maps, rider comparison logic.
- Audio: terminology contrasts, timeline recall cues.
- Reading-heavy holdout: exclusions and nuanced option interactions.

**Cluster B (N3–N4: Operations + Retirement)**
- Video: process workflows and role boundaries.
- Audio: sequence reminders and compliance checkpoints.
- Reading-heavy holdout: retirement treatment nuances.

**Cluster C (N5–N6: Health Core)**
- Video: plan architecture and clause comparisons.
- Audio: cost-sharing and renewability memory prompts.
- Reading-heavy holdout: exception-heavy clause details.

**Cluster D (N7–N8: Social + Integration)**
- Video: integrative scenario walkthroughs.
- Audio: public-program distinctions and fast recall lists.
- Reading-heavy holdout: integrated judgment scenarios.

**Cluster E (TX1–TX4: Texas Law)**
- Video: law-family orientation and distinctions.
- Audio: trigger/timeframe memorization.
- Reading-heavy holdout: black-letter distinctions and exceptions.

### 8.2 Sequence timing rule
For every cluster:
1) read unit,
2) complete mini review,
3) do short video/audio reinforcement,
4) take checkpoint,
5) use media again only for weak tags.

## SECTION 9: MASTERY-EXAM ARCHITECTURE

### 9.1 Number of mastery forms
**3 mastery forms (M1–M3)**
- **M1 Diagnostic:** first full simulation after checkpoint completion.
- **M2 Readiness:** after remediation cycle.
- **M3 Final Confidence (optional but recommended):** near exam week.

### 9.2 Gating condition before mastery
Student must complete CP1–CP5 and meet checkpoint progression thresholds.

### 9.3 Simulation requirements
- timed full-length behavior,
- blueprint-weighted domain mix,
- realistic distractor style,
- combined national + Texas coverage.

### 9.4 Score-band policy
- **<72%:** weak/not ready.
- **72–79%:** borderline; targeted remediation required.
- **≥80%:** likely ready; maintain with mixed short drills.

### 9.5 Remediation protocol after weak mastery
1. generate domain + subtopic deficit report,
2. assign mandatory reread targets,
3. assign QBank remediation sets (20–40/item blocks),
4. assign reinforcement clips,
5. retest on next mastery form.

### 9.6 Mastery vs checkpoint vs QBank
- **Mastery:** final readiness proof.
- **Checkpoint:** staged competency proof.
- **QBank:** iterative precision repair.
